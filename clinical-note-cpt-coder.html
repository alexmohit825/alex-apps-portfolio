    2021 AMA MDM Quick Reference

    MDM is determined by the highest 2 of 3 elements:

    Element 1: Number & Complexity of Problems Addressed

    Element 2: Amount & Complexity of Data Reviewed/Analyzed

    Element 3: Risk of Complications, Morbidity, or Mortality

    The final MDM level equals the second-highest element level (2-of-3 rule).

    Consultation Requirements — The 3 R's

    All consultation codes (99241–99245, 99251–99255) require documentation of all 3 R's:

    R1 — REQUEST: The consult must be requested by another physician or qualified healthcare provider. Document who requested the consult and why.

    R2 — RENDER: The consulting provider must personally evaluate the patient and document their findings, medical decision making, and recommendations.

    R3 — REPORT: The consulting physician must communicate findings and recommendations back to the requesting provider. Document the method and recipient of communication.

    Missing any of the 3 R's may result in claim denial. All three must be clearly documented in the consultation note.

    Time-Based Coding

    If total time on the date of encounter is documented, the visit may be coded by time regardless of MDM complexity. Time includes face-to-face and non-face-to-face activities (chart review, ordering, care coordination, counseling).
