    Wound Care Instructions

    Normal Healing Timeline

    Days 1–3

    Swelling, bruising, and scabbing are normal. Wound may feel tight and tender. Keep the wound dry.

    Days 4–7

    Swelling may peak then start to subside. Itching is a sign of healing. Light pink color at wound edges is normal.

    Days 7–14

    Staple/suture removal typically occurs during this period. Incision edges start to knit together firmly.

    Weeks 2–4

    Scar formation begins. Pink or red appearance at the incision is normal and will gradually fade.

    Months 1–3

    Scar maturation. The scar softens, flattens, and fades over time. Full maturation can take 6–12 months.

    How to Take a Good Monitoring Photo

    📐
    1. Position & Distance

    Hold your phone 6–12 inches from the wound. Try to take the photo at the same angle and distance each time.

    💡
    2. Good Lighting

    Use natural daylight or a well-lit room. Avoid harsh shadows or flash glare directly on the wound.

    📏
    3. Include a Scale Reference

    Place a coin or ruler next to the wound to show size. This helps your surgeon gauge changes.

    🔍
    4. Capture the Full Incision

    Make sure the entire wound is visible in the photo, including surrounding skin for context.
