Clinical Decision Support System

NecessityIQ helps reduce prior authorization denials and reverse unfavorable decisions for indicated and medically reasonable treatment requests.

Follow these simple steps to generate your prior authorization or appeal documentation.

🤖 Works with any AI platform: NecessityIQ output is formatted text that can be pasted into ANY AI platform for letter generation — including Microsoft Copilot Chat, ChatGPT, Claude, Google Gemini, or any other AI assistant. After clicking "Copy Prompt for AI Letter," simply open your preferred AI platform and paste (Ctrl+V) the prompt to receive a tailored medical necessity letter.

For Prior Authorization

1

Select your insurers — Choose the Primary Insurer from the dropdown. If applicable, select a Secondary Insurer.

2

Choose your specialty — Select the relevant medical specialty (e.g., Spine Surgery).

3

Enter CPT codes — Type the CPT codes for your planned procedure(s) separated by commas, or use the quick-add dropdown to select common codes.

4

Paste your clinic note — Copy and paste the full clinic note into the text box. You can also click "Load Sample Note" to test with example data.

5

Run the analysis — Click "Run NecessityIQ Analysis" to see a gap analysis showing which insurer criteria are met or missing.

6

🤖 Generate your letter — Click "Copy Prompt for AI Letter" to copy a formatted prompt to your clipboard. Then manually paste it (Ctrl+V) into any AI platform (Copilot Chat, ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, or other) to receive a tailored, insurer-specific medical necessity letter.

For Appeals

1

Complete steps 1–4 above — Select insurer, specialty, CPT codes, and paste your clinic note in the Appeal tab.

2

Select denial category — Choose the reason for denial from the dropdown.

3

Paste the denial letter — Copy and paste the denial letter content into the denial letter text box.

4

🤖 Generate your appeal — Click "Copy Appeal Prompt for AI" to copy the prompt. Then manually paste it (Ctrl+V) into any AI platform for a comprehensive appeal letter with point-by-point rebuttals.
