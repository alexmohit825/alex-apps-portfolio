🍽️ Fasting & Diet

Critical instructions for your safety

Nothing by Mouth (NPO)

Failure to follow fasting instructions may result in your surgery being cancelled or rescheduled.

Surgery Time

🏥

Nothing by Mouth

Completely empty stomach required

No solid food after midnight the night before surgery (or 8 hours before your scheduled surgery time). This includes all meals, snacks, candy, and chewing gum. Even a small amount of food in your stomach can be dangerous during anesthesia — it can cause aspiration, where stomach contents enter your lungs.

Allowed up to 2 hours before surgery: Water, apple juice (no pulp), black coffee, plain tea (no milk or cream), clear broth, Jell-O (no fruit), Gatorade/Pedialyte.

NOT allowed: Milk, cream, orange juice, smoothies, protein shakes, soup with noodles/vegetables, alcohol.

If you are diabetic, contact your care team for specific fasting and medication instructions. Blood sugar management may require adjusted insulin doses. Some oral diabetes medications must be held the day of surgery. You may still take approved medications with a small sip of water on the morning of surgery — always confirm with your surgical team.

Common Fasting Myths

Can I chew gum?

🚫 No.

Gum stimulates stomach acid production and counts as eating.

Can I have a small snack?

🚫 No.

Even a cracker or candy can delay or cancel your surgery.

Can I drink coffee?

✅ Only black coffee

Before the 2-hour cutoff. No cream, sugar, or flavored creamers.

💊 Medications

Know which medications to continue and which to stop

Important

ALWAYS confirm your specific medication plan with your surgeon or anesthesiologist. This is general guidance only.

Usually Continue

  • ✅ Blood pressure medications
  • ✅ Thyroid medications
  • ✅ Seizure medications
  • ✅ Inhalers (asthma/COPD)
  • ✅ Eye drops

Take with a small sip of water the morning of surgery.

Usually Stop

  • 🚫 Blood thinners (warfarin, aspirin, clopidogrel) — stop 5-7 days prior
  • 🚫 NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) — stop 5-7 days prior
  • 🚫 Diabetes medications — hold day of surgery
  • 🚫 Herbal supplements — stop 2 weeks prior

Stop ALL herbal supplements at least 2 weeks before surgery. These can cause excessive bleeding, interact with anesthesia, or affect heart rate:

Garlic Ginger Ginkgo Biloba St. John's Wort Valerian Echinacea Fish Oil / Omega-3 Vitamin E (high dose) Kava

🛁 Hygiene & Prep

Cleanliness reduces infection risk

🧴 Chlorhexidine (CHG) Bathing

Night Before Surgery

  • Shower with CHG soap (if provided) or regular soap
  • Scrub entire body for at least 3 minutes
  • Do NOT apply lotion, deodorant, perfume, or makeup after shower
  • Sleep on clean sheets with clean pajamas

Morning of Surgery

  • Another CHG shower if instructed
  • Wear clean, loose, comfortable clothes
  • Do NOT apply any products to your skin

💅 Nail Care

  • Remove ALL nail polish (especially index fingers for pulse oximeter)
  • Keep nails short and clean
  • No acrylic or gel nails

💍 Jewelry & Piercings

  • Remove ALL rings, necklaces, bracelets, watches
  • Remove ALL piercings and body jewelry
  • Remove hearing aids (unless directed otherwise)

💇 Hair

  • No hair products (gel, spray, mousse)
  • If having head/neck surgery, wash hair thoroughly

🦷 Dental

  • Remove dentures, bridges, partial plates on day of surgery
  • Inform anesthesia team of loose or capped teeth

🧳 Day of Surgery

Be prepared and arrive with confidence

Transportation Reminder

You CANNOT drive yourself home. Arrange for a responsible adult to drive you and stay with you for 24 hours after surgery.

What to BRING

Leave at HOME

  • Jewelry and valuables
  • Contact lenses (wear glasses instead)
  • Large amounts of cash
  • Unnecessary electronics

Arrival

Arrive 1-2 hours before your scheduled surgery time. Check in at the admissions / surgical reception desk.

😴 Anesthesia Explained

Understanding anesthesia helps ease anxiety

😴

General Anesthesia

You will be completely asleep. A breathing tube may be used. This is most common for major surgeries. You won't feel, see, or remember anything.

💉

Regional Anesthesia

Numbs a large area of your body (spinal, epidural, nerve block). You may be awake or lightly sedated. Common for orthopedic and lower body surgeries.

🌙

Local + Sedation (MAC)

Light sleep and relaxation. The surgical area is numbed locally. You breathe on your own. Used for minor procedures and biopsies.

🩺 Post-Op Recovery

What to expect as you heal

🏥 Hospital Recovery Room (PACU)

After surgery, you'll be taken to the Post-Anesthesia Care Unit where nurses will closely monitor your vitals. Pain is managed with IV medications.

You must meet these criteria to go home (or to a hospital room):

Alert & Awake Stable Vitals Pain Controlled Able to Take Fluids

🏠 Home Recovery

Take prescribed pain medications as directed — don't wait until pain becomes severe. Stay ahead of the pain.

Use ice packs (20 minutes on, 20 minutes off) for swelling. Elevate the surgical area if applicable.

Start walking as early as possible — even short walks help prevent blood clots and speed recovery. No driving for at least 24 hours. No heavy lifting or strenuous exercise until cleared by your surgeon.

Keep incision clean and dry for at least 48 hours. Do not submerge in pools, hot tubs, or baths until fully healed. Watch for signs of infection.

⚠️ Warning Signs

Know when to seek immediate medical attention

Call Your Surgeon's Office

  • 🔶 Fever over 101°F (38.3°C)
  • 🔶 Increasing redness, warmth, or swelling at incision
  • 🔶 Pus or unusual drainage from wound
  • 🔶 Uncontrolled pain not relieved by medications
  • 🔶 Nausea/vomiting preventing fluid intake
  • 🔶 Difficulty urinating for more than 8 hours

CALL 911 / Emergency Room

  • 🔴 Chest pain or pressure
  • 🔴 Difficulty breathing or shortness of breath
  • 🔴 Calf pain with leg swelling (DVT/PE signs)
  • 🔴 Confusion or altered mental status
  • 🔴 Uncontrolled bleeding
